Problem Solving

What is Problem Solving?

“to use cognitive processing to find a solution to a difficult question or situation”
- Many of us focus on the act of finding a solution. - We ask devs how their day went and they tell us about frustrations. - We focus on results. Did you finish it? Did you figure it out? - We get really upset and feel out of this place when we struggle to find a solution

"Difficult question or situation"

  • This process is all about failing and hitting dead ends
  • How else will you get better?

“In order to succeed you must fail, so that you know what not to do the next time.”

Anthony J. D’Angelo

We're all human.

Ask the crowd, how many people were stuck on something this week? How many of you were a little upset - scared/worried while you were stuck? How many of you then criticized yourself for being stuck? Use this to go to next point which is shared experiences & unrealistic expectations on reality.

What causes these feelings of Anxiety?

  • Unrealistic expectations on reality
  • We think we're alone

#1: A good developer knows/does...

- Name same tools/languages/frameworks. - We look at things we don't know and feel that that makes us unefficient developer - Touch on the fact that this is especially strong feeling in our field given the fast-paced environment. -This is a dangerous way of thinking because this only reinforces our expectations on reality, and makes us afraid to try new things in case we fail at them. -This is also reaaallly not fair. -We don't think about what we already know clearly. -To make things even more complicated, our field is filled with advancing technologies and there are always these great people that we learn about. - We look at people around us learners of things old and new and think “these guys and girls know what they’re doing.” I “should know what Im doing”

Instead, we can:

  • Remember that there is no set definition of what a developer 'should' know
  • Be honest about our strengths and weaknesses, and do the same with others
  • Gently encourage yourself to explore and try new things

#2: But person X knows subject Y...

We all know people at work, school, personal lives, or big names/creators of products/companies. We try to think about what they know and all the greatness they have achieved. By doing so we paint a very false picture.

Instead, remember:

  • You know things
  • Other people know things
  • These things overlap far more than you realize
  • You may not be seeing other people very clearly

“The reason we struggle with insecurity is because we compare our behind-the-scenes with everyone else’s highlight reel.”

Steve Furtick
