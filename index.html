Problem Solving

What is Problem Solving?

“to use cognitive processing to find a solution to a difficult question or situation”
- Think this definition is enough.
- Focus is solely on the solution.
- We become obsessed.
- Ask a dev how their day went and they will be sad/frustrated that they haven't solved it yet.
- Makes us feel like being stuck is wrong and abnormal.
- Makes us feel that being stuck is not part of the process.
*click*

Too Much Focus On The Solution

“to use cognitive processing to find a solution to a difficult question or situation”
- The actual defination itself acknowledges that the problem is difficult.
- We need to acknowledge this.
- It's part of the process!

"Difficult question or situation"

  • Being stuck is to be expected
  • Doesn't matter how much you know
  • Reaching dead ends is a Good thing
  • How else will you get better?
- If you're not finding it difficult sometimes, you're doing it wrong!
- It's part of the definition, so don't feel out of place! It's part of being a developer.
- We ALL do this.

We're all human

Ask the crowd, how many people were stuck on something this week? How many of you were a little upset - scared/worried while you were stuck? How many of you then criticized yourself for being stuck? Use this to go to next point which is shared experiences & unrealistic expectations on reality.

What causes these feelings of Anxiety?

  • Unrealistic expectations on reality
  • We think we're alone
  • First bulletpoint was already touched regarding expecations on never being stuck

#1: A good developer knows/does...

  • Name same tools/languages/frameworks.
  • We look at things we don't know and feel that that makes us unefficient developer
  • Touch on the fact that this is especially strong feeling in our field given the fast-paced environment.
  • This is a dangerous way of thinking because this only reinforces our expectations on reality, and makes us afraid to try new things in case we fail at them.
  • This is also reaaallly not fair. -We don't think about what we already know clearly.
  • To make things even more complicated, our field is filled with advancing technologies and there are always these great people that we learn about.
  • We look at people around us learners of things old and new and think “these guys and girls know what they’re doing.” I “should know what Im doing”

Instead, we can:

  • Remember that there is no set definition of what a developer 'should' know
  • Be honest about our strengths and weaknesses, and do the same with others
  • Gently encourage yourself to explore and try new things
No right or wrong - mention difference between specializing at one thing or exploring other things.

#2: But person X knows subject Y...

We all know people at work, school, personal lives, or big names/creators of products/companies. We try to think about what they know and all the greatness they have achieved. By doing so we paint a very false picture.

Instead, remember:

  • You know things
  • Other people know things
  • These things overlap far more than you realize
  • You may not be seeing other people very clearly
